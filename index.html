Artist Statement

Colour and distortion are part of the meaning, not just the effect.

I am interested in what happens when a familiar image is pushed past recognition. By changing colour balance, stretching edges, and reshaping the source through digital tools, I can turn something still into something that feels active and difficult to ignore.

In a visual culture shaped by short videos and fragmented attention, I want a single image to feel powerful enough to slow the viewer down. My work uses visual pressure, contrast, and movement to create that pause.

From reference to transformation

My process often begins with an ordinary object or fragment. The final image emerges through layers of adjustment, disruption, and intensified colour.

Creative Journey

How this body of work reflects the way I think.

This portfolio is not just a gallery. It also shows the ideas and working habits behind the images.

01

Observation

I often begin with ordinary materials and references, looking for shapes that can be stretched beyond their original function.

02

Manipulation

Photoshop becomes a way to test pressure: levels, saturation, colour channels, and wave distortion all push the image into motion.

03

Escalation

Each draft grows louder and more unstable. I keep increasing contrast and movement until the visual energy feels unavoidable.

04

Impact

The final goal is emotional attention: a static image that feels fast, vivid, and memorable enough to invite a second look.
